Butter Naan

The most popular Indian bread worldwide

About Butter Naan

Butter naan holds an esteemed place in the realm of bread for its unparalleled combination of softness, richness, and versatility. This Indian flatbread, generously slathered with butter and cooked in a tandoor oven, captivates with its pillowy texture and irresistible aroma. Each bite offers a delightful blend of savory flavors, enhanced by the indulgent touch of butter. Its ability to complement a wide array of dishes, from creamy curries to smoky kebabs, makes it a beloved choice across cultures. Whether enjoyed plain or stuffed with delectable fillings, butter naan transcends mere sustenance, embodying the essence of comfort and culinary delight.

The Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Yeast
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Yogurt
  • Milk
  • Butter (for dough and spreading)
  • Garlic (optional, for garlic naan)
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander leaves, optional, for garnish)

Lets Cook

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, yeast, sugar, and salt.
  2. Add yogurt and milk to the dry ingredients, then knead into a soft dough.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest in a warm place for about 1 hour, allowing it to rise.
  4. After the dough has risen, divide it into small portions and roll each portion into a ball.
  5. Roll out each ball of dough into a flat oval or round shape using a rolling pin.
  6. Heat a tawa (flat skillet) or a tandoor oven until hot.
  7. Place the rolled naan on the hot tawa or in the tandoor and cook until bubbles start to appear on the surface.
  8. Flip the naan and cook the other side until it puffs up and gets golden brown spots.
  9. Remove the naan from the heat and brush it generously with melted butter.
  10. If desired, sprinkle minced garlic and chopped cilantro over the buttered naan for added flavor.
  11. Serve the butter naan hot with your favorite curry or dish.
